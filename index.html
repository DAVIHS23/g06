Background information and initial idea

This website is a project from the HSLU course "Data Visualization (DAVI)". The goal is to create a functional visualization of a dataset of our choice. Our team decided to use the IMDB Top 10'000 movies dataset available on Kaggle.

This visualization helps investors, producers, actors and movie enthusiasts to find new genre and movie trends, so that they are able to make informed decisions. The initial idea consisted of basic digital sketches of the different visualizations. These ideas were refined over the semester and lead to the current version of this website.

References

The main source for this project is the IMDB Top 10'000 movies dataset released on Kaggle. The dataset contains information about the movies, such as the title, the year of release, the genre, the actors, the runtime, the ratings and the income. The income can be missing for some movies.
Additionally, the movie posters and the actor images are loaded from the The Movie DB API .

Visual Design Choices

1. Treemap

The visualization's design is thoughtfully chosen to align with established principles. It employs foundational visual design concepts, using color and space efficiently to differentiate data and enhance user interaction. The high data-ink ratio respects Tufte's principles, avoiding nonessential decorative elements and focusing on data representation. In tune with human perception, the familiar formats of scatterplots and bar charts facilitate quick understanding and insight. Designed for specific tasks in data visualization, it allows for comparative analysis, trend observation, and detailed examination of individual data points. Lastly, the interaction concept is well integrated, inviting users to engage with the data through filtering, clicking, and brushing to explore deeper narratives within the data set.

2. Treemap

The treemap uses size to reflect box office success, with interactive titles revealing movie posters for added context without clutter, embracing Tufte's data depth ideal. A single-color scheme focuses attention on revenue, in line with Tufte's data-ink ratio, simplifying interpretation. A "Low to High Income" legend aids quick income level assessment, while the genre filter enhances data exploration and comparison, streamlining user interaction and visual clarity.

3. Linecharts

This line chart pairing contrasts movie release volume and average income over time, enhanced by a genre filter and ratings slider for customized data exploration. The minimalist design with clear trend lines and noted data bounds maximizes data visibility and user clarity, in line with Tufte's principles.
